Palak Paneer

Description

Palak Paneer is a beloved Indo-Chinese dish that perfectly balances bold flavors with a touch of spice. Featuring crispy paneer cubes tossed in a vibrant sauce of soy, chili, and garlic, this dish is a crowd-pleaser that works as both an appetizer and a main course. Whether you prefer it dry for a finger-licking snack or with a gravy to pair with fried rice or noodles, Chili Paneer is a versatile delight that's sure to tantalize your taste buds. Dive into this easy-to-make recipe and bring the restaurant-style flavors straight to your table!

Ingredients

    For Paneer Preparation:

    • 250g paneer, cut into cubes
    • 3 tablespoons cornflour
    • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour (maida)
    • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper powder
    • 1/4 teaspoon salt
    • Water (as needed)
    • Oil (for frying)

    For Sauce:

    • 1 tablespoon oil
    • 1 teaspoon finely chopped garlic
    • 1 teaspoon finely chopped ginger
    • 2 green chilies, slit or finely chopped
    • 1 medium onion, diced
    • 1 medium capsicum (bell pepper), diced
    • 2-3 tablespoons soy sauce
    • 1 tablespoon red chili sauce
    • 1 tablespoon tomato ketchup
    • 1/2 teaspoon vinegar
    • 1 teaspoon sugar (optional, to balance flavors)
    • Salt (to taste)
    • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper powder

    For Garnish:

    • Spring onion greens, chopped

Steps

    Step 1: Preparing the Paneer

    1. In a bowl, mix cornflour, all-purpose flour, black pepper powder, and salt.
    2. Add water gradually to form a smooth, thick batter.
    3. Dip paneer cubes into the batter, ensuring they're well-coated.
    4. Heat oil in a frying pan and deep-fry or shallow-fry the coated paneer cubes until golden and crispy. Remove and set aside on a paper towel to drain excess oil.

    Step 2: Preparing the Sauce

    1. Heat 1 tablespoon oil in a wok or large pan over medium heat.
    2. Add chopped garlic, ginger, and green chilies. Saut√© until fragrant.
    3. Toss in diced onions and capsicum. Stir-fry for 2-3 minutes until slightly tender but still crunchy.

    Step 3: Making the Sauce

    1. Add soy sauce, red chili sauce, tomato ketchup, vinegar, and sugar (if using). Mix well.
    2. Season with salt and black pepper powder. Adjust sauces to taste.

    Step 4: Combining the Paneer

    1. Add the fried paneer cubes to the sauce. Toss gently to coat them evenly.
    2. If you prefer a thicker sauce, mix 1 teaspoon of cornflour with 2 tablespoons of water to make a slurry, and add it to the pan. Cook for 1-2 minutes until the sauce thickens slightly.

    Step 5: Garnishing and Serving

    1. Turn off the heat and garnish with chopped spring onion greens.
    2. Serve hot as an appetizer or pair it with fried rice or noodles for a complete meal.
